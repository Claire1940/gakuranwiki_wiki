<Section icon={Swords} title="Gakuran Fighting Styles & Customization">

In Gakuran, your fighting style defines how you engage in combat, while your Yen-purchased outfits define how you look while doing it. The game features three core fighting styles that can be rolled upon character creation or rerolled for 5 Robux from the Stats menu. Understanding the strengths and weaknesses of each style is crucial, as your combat efficiency directly impacts how fast you farm Yen.

Let's break down the styles in depth. Boxing is the ultimate defensive baseline. Its punch chains are tightly animated, allowing you to quickly transition from an offensive string into a solid block. Muay Thai, on the other hand, is built for pure aggression. It utilizes powerful knee strikes and roundhouse kicks to crack an opponent's guard and maintain forward momentum. Hakari is a highly unique, off-meta style inspired by the legendary jackpot mechanics of Hakari Kinji. It features flashy, unpredictable animations that can easily confuse opponents who rely on standard parry timings. Choosing the right style is the first step toward dominating the arena and securing a massive Yen income.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Combat Mechanics & Grinding Loops">

To survive the open-world chaos of Gakuran and protect your hard-earned Yen, you must master the game's core melee mechanics. The combat is heavily skill-focused, relying on precise timing, parries, and reactive movement rather than mindless button-mashing.

The absolute foundation of Gakuran combat is the **punch-punch-slam-stomp** combo. Landing this sequence cleanly will knock down your opponent, leaving them vulnerable to an execution or a quick getaway. Additionally, you must master the **weaving** mechanic. By holding Space and pressing a movement key, your character will perform a swift dodge, allowing you to slip past incoming attacks and immediately launch a devastating counter-strike.

Stamina management is the silent decider of every battle in Gakuran. Every dash, dodge, and heavy attack drains your stamina bar. If you deplete your stamina entirely, your character will enter a fatigued state, leaving you unable to block or weave. This is when opponents will land their devastating punch-punch-slam-stomp combos. Furthermore, the community has developed a strict delinquent culture, including the infamous 'Walk of Shame.' When you are knocked out, rival gang members can carry your body and parade you around the campus before executing you. To avoid this public embarrassment and protect your Yen, always keep an eye on your stamina, utilize parries to stun aggressive attackers, and never fight with your back to a wall.
