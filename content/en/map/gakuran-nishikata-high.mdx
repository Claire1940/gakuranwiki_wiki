<Section icon={Target} title="PvP Combat Guide and Strategies">

Combat in **gakuran nishikata high** is a game of patience and baiting. Reckless swinging (often called "M1 spamming") will lead to a quick defeat against any player who understands the parry mechanic. Follow these steps to improve your win rate in open-world school fights.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Enter Combat Stance Early">
    Never walk into a potential conflict zone without pressing **R**. The combat stance reduces your movement speed slightly but readies your block and dash, preventing you from being caught in a "first-hit" stunlock.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Master the Dash-Dance">
    Use **Q** to dash laterally rather than directly away. Most attacks in the game have forward momentum; by dashing to the side, you stay within range to counter-attack while the opponent is still in their whiff animation.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Confirm the Parry">
    Wait for the visual or audio cue of an opponent's swing before pressing **F**. A successful parry leaves the enemy vulnerable for approximately 1.5 seconds—enough time to land a full light attack chain or a single heavy finisher.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Reset and Reposition">
    After landing a combo, do not immediately chase. Back off, regain your stamina, and look for the next opening. Constant aggression leads to stamina depletion, which makes you a stationary target.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Advanced Tactic: The Wall Pin">
In the narrow hallways of the school, try to position your opponent against a wall or locker. This restricts their ability to dash sideways, forcing them to either block (which you can break with a heavy) or take the full force of your combo.
</Callout>

Effective combat also involves knowing when to leave. If you are outnumbered in the basketball court or the gym, use the environment—stairs and doorways—to break line of sight and escape. There is no shame in tactical retreat in a sandbox brawler.

</Section>
