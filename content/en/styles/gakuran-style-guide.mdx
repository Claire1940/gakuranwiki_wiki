<Section icon={Swords} title="Gakuran Style Guide: Fighting Styles Tier List">

Choosing your combat discipline is the most critical decision you will make. This **gakuran style guide** segment breaks down the current fighting styles based on pressure tools, ease of use, mobility, and overall PvP viability. The game currently features three distinct styles: Muay Thai, Boxing, and the off-meta Hakari style. Each discipline caters to a specific playstyle, meaning your choice will dictate how you approach spacing, block discipline, and offensive pressure.

While some players prefer the flashy, unpredictable nature of off-meta choices, competitive success in Gakuran heavily favors styles with reliable frame data and strong forward momentum. The table below compares these styles to help you choose the best route before dedicating hours to combo practice.

| Style | Tier | Role | Key Strength | Practice Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Muay Thai** | **S-Tier** | Aggressive Striker | Kick-based melee, fast punishes | Dash-in attacks, block timing |
| **Boxing** | **A-Tier** | Reliable Brawler | Clean punch chains, high defense | Light attacks, safe disengages |
| **Hakari** | **B-Tier** | Off-Meta Specialist | High surprise value, flashy moves | Baiting blocks, matchup awareness |

<Callout type="tip" title="Style Rerolling">
If you find yourself struggling against aggressive players, consider switching to Boxing. Its straightforward defensive options and readable punch strings make it the absolute best tool for learning core spacing mechanics.
</Callout>

### Muay Thai (S-Tier)
Muay Thai is the undisputed king of close-range pressure. Utilizing powerful kick-based melee attacks, this style excels at breaking through opponent guards and delivering rapid punishes. It is highly effective in crowded hallway fights where chaotic brawling is common. However, it requires precise spacing; reckless chasing will leave you vulnerable to quick counter-attacks.

### Boxing (A-Tier)
Boxing is the most beginner-friendly style in Gakuran. It offers highly reliable punch combos and excellent block recovery times. Players using Boxing can easily execute light punch chains and transition smoothly into defensive disengages. While it lacks the raw range of Muay Thai, its defensive consistency makes it an incredibly safe pick for solo players.

### Hakari (B-Tier)
Hakari is a flashy, off-meta style designed for players who value creative expression over standard pressure routes. It features unique attack angles and high visual flair, making it excellent for baiting opponents who are unfamiliar with its specific matchup timings. However, it requires deep game knowledge and is not recommended for beginners.

<Section icon={Target} title="Combat Progression & Onboarding Path">

Transitioning from a peaceful roleplayer to a competent schoolyard fighter requires a structured approach to learning. Do not simply wander into the main hallways expecting to win button-mashing contests. Follow this step-by-step progression path to build your muscle memory and establish reliable combat habits.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initialize Your Settings">
    Launch the game from the official Roblox experience page. Before starting any fight, open your in-game settings menu to adjust your graphics levels for maximum frame rate. Enable Shift Lock and familiarize yourself with the camera behavior.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Toggle Your Combat Stance">
    Never approach an opponent with your hands down. Press **R** (or your platform's equivalent) to enter your active combat stance. This posture prepares your character to execute immediate blocks, dashes, and light attacks without delay.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Practice Safe Spacing">
    Head to the Gym or Workout Area. Practice dashing in with **Q**, executing a short light-attack chain, and immediately dashing out. Avoid chasing opponents in straight lines; instead, cut angles around pillars and corners to disrupt their camera tracking.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Master the Block-and-Parry Loop">
    Find a sparring partner and focus entirely on defense. Hold **F** to block incoming attacks, paying close attention to the visual and audio cues of your opponent's swings. Release and re-press block right as an attack lands to trigger a perfect parry.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Incorporate Grabs and Executes">
    Once an opponent is downed, press **B** to carry them out of active danger zones, or use **V** to execute a grab-finish. This ensures they cannot be easily revived by their teammates during group skirmishes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Combo Principle">
In Gakuran, short and reliable strings will always beat overly complex, flashy combos. Focus on executing two light attacks, observing your opponent's reaction, and repositioning rather than committing to a full, easily punishable auto-combo.
</Callout>

</Section>
