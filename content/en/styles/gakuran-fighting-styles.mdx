<Section icon={Zap} title="Detailed Style Breakdown and Mechanics">

Each style in Gakuran dictates your movement speed, attack range, and recovery frames. Choosing a style isn't just about damage; it's about how you manage the space between you and your opponent.

### Muay Thai: The King of Pressure
Muay Thai is designed for players who want to dictate the pace of the fight. It utilizes a mix of heavy-hitting kicks and quick knee strikes that can break an opponent's guard if they are too passive. The style excels in "hotspot" fights where multiple players are involved, as its lateral movement allows for quick target switching.

### Boxing: The Reliable Foundation
Boxing is the "bread and butter" of Gakuran. It features clean, horizontal punch chains that are easy to confirm. New players should start here to learn the rhythm of M1 (Light Attack) and M2 (Heavy Attack) sequences. Its defensive recovery is slightly faster than Muay Thai, making it more forgiving if you miss a swing.

### Hakari: The Specialist's Choice
Hakari is less about raw power and more about psychological warfare. It requires deep matchup knowledge because its win condition relies on baiting blocks and using unconventional movement to get behind the opponent's camera. It is not recommended for those who haven't mastered basic dash-cancel mechanics.

<Callout type="success" title="Pro Strategy">
Use the **Gym / Workout Area** to test the specific range of your style's M2 attack. Knowing exactly where your hitboxes end will prevent you from whiffing and leaving yourself open to a counter-combo.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering Combat: Controls and Keybinds">

To excel in Gakuran, you must move beyond the default Roblox movement. The game utilizes a specific set of combat inputs that allow for advanced maneuvers like dashing, parrying, and executing downed opponents.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Enter Combat Stance">
    Press **R** on your keyboard to toggle your stance. This changes your idle animation and prepares your character for instant blocking and attacking. You cannot parry effectively without being in this stance.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Master the Dash">
    Use **Q** to dodge or dash. Dashing is your primary tool for "whiff punishing"—waiting for an opponent to miss an attack and then immediately closing the gap to start your own combo.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Block-Parry Cycle">
    Hold **F** to block. If you time your block perfectly as an attack hits, you perform a parry, which briefly stuns the opponent and gives you a free opening for a heavy attack (M2).
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Finish the Fight">
    Once an opponent is knocked down, use **V** to grab or execute. This ensures they cannot quickly recover and rejoin the fight, which is crucial in group brawls.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Warning">
Avoid "panic blocking" from a distance. Experienced players will wait for you to hold **F**, then dash in with a guard-breaking heavy attack or a grab, leaving you completely defenseless.
</Callout>
